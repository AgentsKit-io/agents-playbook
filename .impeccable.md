## Design Context

### Users

Engineering teams adopting coding agents, maintainers standardizing multiple repositories, and coding agents retrieving production rules. They need to move from a real failure mode to a practical pattern, copy-ready template, or executable gate quickly, while retaining access to complete evidence when needed.

### Brand Personality

Experienced, rigorous, and pragmatic. Agents Playbook should feel production-earned rather than theoretical: calm authority, direct language, and proof before promotion.

### Aesthetic Direction

An editorial engineering manual in the AgentsKit ecosystem, using structured flows, meaningful diagrams, code, and varied information density. Dark mode may remain primary, but contrast and reduced motion are mandatory. Prefer asymmetric explanation and progressive disclosure over decorative dashboards. Avoid neon AI gradients, ornamental glass, repetitive equal-card grids, generic metrics heroes, and effects that do not explain a rule.

### Design Principles

1. Begin with the prevented failure and end with an enforceable outcome.
2. Keep the first reading path concise; place full contracts in raw, llms-full, and reference layers.
3. Use diagrams, examples, and runnable gates whenever they replace explanatory prose.
4. Route framework construction to AgentsKit, chat surfaces to AgentsKit Chat, documentation infrastructure to Doc Bridge, review to Code Review, and enterprise operations to AKOS.
5. Preserve 44px interactions, keyboard access, readable code, and 375–1440px layouts.
